Psychology of Play

What good comes from data alone if it does not push us to further understand ourselves?
Together with my PhD students of the Lux project, I investigated in how far we can measure resilience, the core competence of bouncing back from life's struggles and adversities, using in-game activity and communication data. We built multiple Alternate Reality games to derive collaborative problem solving solutions {{add $counter 1 }}, investigate the applicability of different stressor types {{add $counter 2 }}, attributed for differences in personality {{add $counter 4 }}, and envisioned intervention mechanisms based on emotion regulation and coping strategies {{add $counter 5 }}. Eventually, player personality can not only be predictive {{add $counter 4 }}, but also being predicted by in-game choices and play styles {{add $counter 3 }}, which we furthermore utilized to tailor game mechanics to the very player, as a catalyst for increased intrinsic motivation {{add $counter 10 }}.
